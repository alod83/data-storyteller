Research, teaching, and narrative craft.

Angelica Lo Duca, PhD, is a researcher at the Institute of Informatics and Telematics of the National Research Council, Italy. She is also an adjunct professor of Data Journalism at the University of Pisa.

Her work spans data storytelling, data science, data journalism, data engineering, and web applications. She has also worked on network security, semantic web, linked data, and blockchain, and has published more than 60 scientific papers in conferences and journals.

Beyond research, she writes to make technical ideas more human, memorable, and actionable. Become a Great Data Storyteller brings that mission into a practical framework for professionals who want their data to move people, not just inform them.
